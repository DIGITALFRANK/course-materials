\prob{1} (5 x 4 points) 
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a) ] Give the lengths of the three sides of the triangle formed by the vectors $v$, $w$, and $v-w$, for $v$ and $w$ given below.
\item[(b) ] Give $\cos \theta$, where $\theta$ is the angle between the vectors $v$ and $w$. 
\item[(c) ] Show that the triangle inequality holds for this triangle.
\item[(d) ] Show, numerically, that the unit vectors in the direction of $v$ and $w$ also make the angle $\theta$ found in (b).
\end{itemize}
(Hint: draw some pictures to help.)
\[ v =\cvv{4}{2}, \,\, w = \cvv{2}{3} \]

\prob{2} (5 x 3 points)
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a) ] Write the system of linear equations as a matrix-vector equation, labeling each term.
\item[(b) ] Rewrite (a) in ``column view'' (as a linear combination of columns), labeling each term.
\item[(c) ] Rewrite (a) in ``row view'' (as dot products of rows), labeling each term.
\end{itemize}
(Hint: make sure every variable is represented.)
\begin{align*}
3x - 2y + z & = 6 \\
8x \,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\,\, - 4z & = 2
\end{align*}

\pagebreak

\headerq

\prob{3} (5 x 3 points)
Given the matrices 
\[ A = \left( \begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 6 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 & 10 \end{array} \right), \,\,\,\,\, E = \left( \begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 3 & 0 & 1 \end{array} \right), \]
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a) ] What is $EA$? 
\item[(b) ] What is $AE$?
\item[(c) ] What is $3A - 5E$? 
\end{itemize}
