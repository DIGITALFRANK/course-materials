\prob{1} (5 x 4 points) \\
Let $A$ be a $3 \times 3$ matrix.
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a) ] What is the matrix $P$ that makes the product $PA$ switch rows 1 and 3 of $A$?
\item[(b) ] What is the matrix $E$ that makes the product $EA$ scale row 2 of $A$ by 5?
\item[(c) ] What is the matrix $F^t$ that makes the product $AF$ add 6 times column 3 of $A$ to column 2?
\item[(d) ] What is the matrix $G^{-1}$ that makes the product $AG$ scale column 1 of $A$ by -4?
\end{itemize}
Hint: Try examples on a sample $3 \times 3$ matrix, such as 
\[ A = \mrrr{4}{-3}{2}{8}{6}{0}{0}{5}{-1}. \]

\prob{2} (10 x 3 points) \\
Consider the linear system $A\vec{x} = b$ given by the equations 
\begin{align*}
4x + 8y + 5z & = 10 \\
8x - 2y + 3z & = 18 \\
2x - 7y - 2z & = 5.
\end{align*}
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a) ] Solve the system of equations. 
\item[(b) ] Give the $LDU$ factorization of the system's coefficient matrix.
\item[(c) ] Give the inverse of the system's coefficient matrix.
\end{itemize}
(Hint: $L$ and $U$ should have 1 on their diagonals, and if you follow the elimination/substitution method, you'll do all three parts of the problem simultaneously. Be sure ALL answers are CLEARLY written and labeled. Check your answers.)
